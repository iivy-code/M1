📘 Grammar Summary

1. Present Simple

Affirmative: Subject + verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)

I play football. → She plays football.

Negative: Subject + don't/doesn't + verb

I don't like tea. → He doesn't like tea.

Question: Do/Does + subject + verb?

Do you like tea? → Does she like tea?

Use it for: routines, habits, facts, and things that are always/usually true.

2. Adverbs of Frequency

alwaysusuallyoftensometimeshardly evernever
100%~90%~70%~40%~10%0%

Position: before the main verb, but after the verb "be".

I always brush my teeth. → She is never late.

3. Prepositions of Time

in — months, years, parts of the day → in July, in 2020, in the morning

on — days, dates → on Monday, on 5th May

at — clock times, night, the weekend → at 7 am, at night, at the weekend
